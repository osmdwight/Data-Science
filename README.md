* ##### MachineLearning for Customer Satisfaction Prediction in Marketing and Advertising

The rapid growth of e-commerce has increased the need for retailers to understand and predict customer satisfaction to support data-driven managerial decisions. Most E-commerce platforms publicly display customer ratings, and these ratings influence  customer decisions, seller credibility, and overall market performance. This is why having a good rating is a must for all sellers, not just to effectively market their products or service but also to hit their business metric goals. However, while e-commerce platforms provide business KPI dashboards to inform sellers about their performance, they do not offer tools that can predict customer ratings before they occur.

The goal of this research is to fill the gap in existing e-commerce systems by **providing a predictive framework that can estimate future customer satisfaction outcomes**. This study analyzed 113,131 orders from 2016 to 2018 made at multiple marketplaces in Brazil listed at Olist Store, a Brazilian e-commerce platform.Customer rating was classified into three groups with rating of _1-2 = 0 (Unsatisfied), 3 = 1 (Neutral) and 4-5 = 2 (Satisfied)_ .The core of the research method involved the application of these  machine learning algorithms:**K-Nearest Neighbors(KNN), Decision Trees, Logistic Regression, AdaBoost, Naive Bayes, LightGBM , CatBoost and Random Forests.** 

The models are evaluated  to determine what is the best model that can capture the underlying pattern of customer satisfaction ratings. Results show that**XGBoost with hyperparameter tuning of  learning_rate= 0.2, max_depth= 8, achieved the highest performance outperforming other models**. The research results indicated product being delivered on time , delivery duration and product price are the three most important factors affecting shopping satisfaction. These findings provide guidance for what area of business should the seller focus on to improve customer satisfaction rating.
